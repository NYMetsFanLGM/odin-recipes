Roast Pork

Description

This is some roast pork I made previously at home. before cooking in the oven, I prepared it overnight.

Ingredients:

  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • 2 teaspoons ground pepper
  • 2 tablespoon adobo with pepper
  • 2 packets of Sason seasoning

Instructions

  1. prepare the garlic powder, oregano, and ground pepper in abowl
  2. place the olive oil on top of the ingredients and mix until the ingredients stick together
  3. cut 1 inch holes within the meat and cut in between the skin and the meat to loosen the skin
  4. place the mixed incredients in the open holes and use any leftover ingredients to cover up the rest of the meat
  5. cover the meat with 1 tablespoon of Adobo and 1 teaspoon on the skin
  6. use 1 packet of Sason seasoning on the meat and 1 packet on the skin
  7. cover pork in baking pan tightly in aluminum foil and place in refridgerator overnight
  8. heat over to 350 degrees while leaving roast pork outside of fridge for around 20 minutes
  9. leave the pork in oven covered for 1 hour per pound. For example, 8 lb. roast pork should stay covered in oven for 8 hours
  10. Remove pork from oven while leaving it at 350 degrees, remove aluminum foil cover on top of pork and put back in oven for 20 more minutes

Roast Ham

Description

this is a roast ham I made previously for Chistmas for my relatives. I was able to find the recipe from this website ham

There are a couple of ingredient differences between what I used and what the website suggests. While I used orange juice, which is suggeted if preferred over pineapple juice, I used ground cinnamon instead of ground ginger and used honey mustard instead of dijon mustard.

Ingredients for Ham:

  • ham
  • 2 tablespoons honey mustard
  • 1/4 cup of orange juice

Ingredients for glaze:

  • 1/2 cup of orange juice
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons honey mustard
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 teaspoons of cornstarch

instructions

  1. preheat oven to 325 degrees
  2. combine honey mustard and orange juice and brush over ham
  3. place ham meat side down in baking pan and cover in aluminum foil
  4. cook for 15 minutes per pound
  5. while ham is cooking, mix glaze ingredients in pot and cook until it boils. remove from heat once finished
  6. When the ham is 15 minutes away from being finished, remove from oven and raise temperature to 425 degrees
  7. remove foil from top of ham, brush glaze all over ham, and place back in oven for remaining 15 minutes
